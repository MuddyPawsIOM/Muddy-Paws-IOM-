Day Care

What a good daycare looks like

Safe groups
Supervised play
Rest time
Clean environment
  • Small, well-managed groups (not chaos)
  • Dogs matched by size/temperament/play style
  • Breaks and quiet time built in
  • Clear pick-up/drop-off procedures

Questions to ask

  • What’s your maximum number of dogs per staff member?
  • Do you do an assessment / trial session?
  • How do you handle scuffles or over-arousal?
  • Are dogs ever left unsupervised?
  • What vaccinations / parasite control do you require?

Signs it might not be the right fit

  • Your dog comes home consistently stressed, hoarse, or overly exhausted
  • Little transparency (no policies, no updates, vague answers)
  • Overcrowding or poor hygiene
